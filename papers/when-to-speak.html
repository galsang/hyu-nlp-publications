When to Speak, When to Abstain: Contrastive Decoding with Abstention

ACL 2025
Hyuhng Joon Kim, Youna Kim, Sang-goo Lee, Taeuk Kim

One-Line Summary

A training-free decoding method that enables LLMs to generate responses when knowledge is available and abstain when it isn't, by adaptively weighting parametric and contextual knowledge sources.

Overview

Background

LLMs sometimes generate confident but wrong answers when they lack knowledge. CDA (Contrastive Decoding with Abstention) addresses this by assessing whether the model has relevant knowledge before answering.

Method: CDA Framework

  • Knowledge Assessment: Evaluate availability of parametric and contextual knowledge
  • Adaptive Weighting: Entropy-based calibration to fairly compare knowledge sources
  • Momentum Decoding: Stabilize weights via momentum: wt ← α·wt-1 + (1-α)·wt

Results

Dataset Method F1ans F1abs RS
NQ CDA-m 73.15 55.47 63.72
HotpotQA CDA-m 79.32 62.59 70.64
TriviaQA CDA-m 80.93 65.66 72.74

Why It Matters

For trustworthy AI, knowing when NOT to answer is as important as giving correct answers. CDA achieves this without any additional training.
