Enhancing Out-of-Distribution Detection in Natural Language Understanding via Implicit Layer Ensemble

EMNLP 2022 Findings
Hyunsoo Cho, Choonghyun Park, Jaewook Kang, Kang Min Yoo, Taeuk Kim, Sang-goo Lee

One-Line Summary

Enhancing out-of-distribution detection in NLU by implicitly ensembling intermediate transformer layer representations.

Background & Motivation

When deploying NLU models in the real world, inputs inevitably arrive that fall outside the training data distribution -- so-called out-of-distribution (OOD) inputs. Models that silently produce confident but incorrect predictions on such inputs pose serious reliability and safety risks. Existing OOD detection methods for NLU typically rely on the final-layer representations or output logits of a transformer model. However, different transformer layers capture different levels of linguistic abstraction, and valuable OOD-discriminative signals may exist in intermediate layers that are discarded when only the final layer is used.

Method

The paper proposes an implicit layer ensemble strategy for OOD detection:

  • Multi-layer representation extraction: Instead of relying solely on the final transformer layer, the method extracts hidden representations from multiple intermediate layers of the pre-trained model
  • Implicit ensemble via aggregation: These multi-layer representations are aggregated through a lightweight mechanism to form a unified OOD score, effectively ensembling the decisions of different layers without training separate models
  • Compatibility with existing detectors: The approach is designed as a plug-in enhancement compatible with various existing OOD scoring functions (e.g., Mahalanobis distance, energy scores), improving them by providing richer representation signals
  • No additional training overhead: The method leverages representations already computed during standard forward passes, requiring minimal additional computation

Results

  • Consistent improvements: The implicit layer ensemble consistently improves OOD detection performance across multiple NLU benchmarks compared to final-layer-only baselines
  • Broad compatibility: Performance gains are observed when combined with various OOD scoring methods, demonstrating the generality of the approach
  • Complementary layer information: Analysis shows that different layers capture different aspects of OOD-ness, validating the hypothesis that intermediate representations carry valuable discriminative signals
  • Maintained in-distribution performance: The enhanced OOD detection does not degrade the model's accuracy on in-distribution examples

Why It Matters

Reliable OOD detection is a prerequisite for trustworthy NLU system deployment. This work reveals that substantial OOD-discriminative information is hidden in intermediate transformer layers and that a simple ensemble over layers can unlock it. The plug-in nature of the method means it can be readily combined with future OOD detection advances. By improving the ability to identify when a model's prediction should not be trusted, this work contributes to building safer and more reliable NLP applications in production settings.
