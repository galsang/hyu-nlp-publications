KGMEL: Knowledge Graph-Enhanced Multimodal Entity Linking

SIGIR 2025
Juyeon Kim, Geon Lee, Taeuk Kim, Kijung Shin

One-Line Summary

Improving multimodal entity linking performance by leveraging knowledge graphs

Background & Motivation

Multimodal Entity Linking (MEL) aims to link textual mentions, accompanied by visual context such as images, to corresponding entities in a knowledge base. While prior approaches rely primarily on textual and visual similarities, they largely overlook the rich structural information stored in knowledge graphs (KGs). This structural information -- relationships and attributes encoded as triples -- can be highly discriminative for distinguishing visually or textually similar entities. KGMEL addresses this gap by systematically integrating KG triples into every stage of the MEL pipeline.

Method: Three-Stage Framework

  • Stage 1 -- Generation: Vision-language models (VLMs) generate knowledge graph triples for each mention by analyzing both textual and visual context. These triples capture structured facts about the entity referenced in the mention.
  • Stage 2 -- Retrieval: Joint mention-entity representations are built through contrastive learning that fuses textual, visual, and triple-based embeddings. These joint embeddings are used to retrieve K candidate entities from the knowledge base.
  • Stage 3 -- Reranking: Irrelevant KG triples are filtered out, and large language models (LLMs) are applied to determine the best-matching entity among the retrieved candidates by comparing mention triples against candidate entity triples.

Results

KGMEL demonstrates consistent improvements over existing MEL methods by effectively leveraging the structural information in knowledge graphs. The three-stage design allows each component to contribute complementary signals, with the KG triple integration proving especially beneficial when visual or textual cues alone are ambiguous.

Why It Matters

Entity linking is a foundational task for information retrieval, question answering, and knowledge-grounded dialogue systems. By showing that KG structure can substantially improve multimodal entity linking, KGMEL opens new directions for integrating structured knowledge into multimodal NLP pipelines.
