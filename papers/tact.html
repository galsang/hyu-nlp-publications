Beyond Task-Oriented and Chitchat Dialogues: Proactive and Transition-Aware Conversational Agents

EMNLP 2025
Yejin Yoon, Yuri Son, Namyoung So, Minseo Kim, Minsoo Cho, Chanhee Park, Seungshin Lee, Taeuk Kim

One-Line Summary

We propose the TACT dataset and novel evaluation metrics (Switch, Recovery) that model natural transitions between task-oriented dialogues (TOD) and chitchat, enabling more flexible and proactive conversational AI.

Background & Motivation

In human conversation, we naturally transition from "Book me a flight" (task-oriented) to "The weather's been great lately" (chitchat). However, existing dialogue AI systems were designed to handle only one or the other.

Limitation: Existing systems are specialized for either task-oriented dialogue (TOD) or chitchat, and cannot handle the mode transitions that naturally occur in real conversations.

Innovation: TACT recognizes that not only users but also agents should proactively be able to switch conversation modes.

TACT Dataset

  • User-Initiated Transitions: Patterns where users suddenly switch to chitchat or return to task-oriented dialogue
  • Agent-Initiated Transitions: Patterns where agents proactively switch conversation modes — a scenario not covered by existing datasets
  • Structural Diversity: Includes diverse transition flows to support modeling of complex dialogue dynamics

Novel Evaluation Metrics

S
Switch
Ability to accurately detect mode transition points and respond appropriately
R
Recovery
Ability to naturally return to the original mode after a transition

Experimental Results

MetricPerformance
Joint Mode-Intent Accuracy75.74%
Human Eval Win Rate vs GPT-4o70.1%
  • Effect of DPO: Applying Direct Preference Optimization on top of a model trained with TACT data further improves performance
  • Intent Detection: Outperforms existing baselines in both intent detection and mode transition handling
  • Human Evaluation: Beats GPT-4o with a 70.1% win rate, receiving high marks for both response quality and transition control
  • Synergy of Structural Diversity + DPO: The combination of data diversity and DPO training is key — neither alone is sufficient

Why It Matters

To create truly natural conversational AI, we must move beyond viewing task-oriented and casual dialogue as separate domains. TACT is the first dataset to model conversational fluidity, enabling AI assistants to learn to flexibly switch conversation modes like humans while not losing track of the original task.
