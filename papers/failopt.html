Investigating the Influence of Prompt-Specific Shortcuts in AI Generated Text Detection

arXiv 2024
Choonghyun Park, Hyuhng Joon Kim, Junyeob Kim, Youna Kim, Taeuk Kim, Hyunsoo Cho, Hwiyeol Jo, Sang-goo Lee, Kang Min Yoo

One-Line Summary

We reveal that AI-generated text detectors are vulnerable to "prompt-specific shortcuts" caused by limited prompt diversity in training data, and propose both the FAILOpt attack that exploits this vulnerability and a defense method that leverages it.

Background & Motivation

With the emergence of ChatGPT and similar tools, detecting AI-written text has become a critical challenge for academia and education. However, if the training data for detectors lacks prompt diversity, "shortcuts" can form where detectors rely on prompt characteristics rather than text content.

Key Insight: Detectors were not actually judging "whether this text was written by AI" but rather responding to "whether this text was generated with a specific prompt."

Proposed Method: FAILOpt

FAILOpt is a 2-stage technique that iteratively searches for prompts that fool detectors:

1
Candidate Generation
Analyze differences between LLM and human outputs to generate instructions that make AI text more human-like
2
Instruction Selection
Select the most effective instructions at fooling detectors from generated candidates (6 iterations, top-2 selection per iteration)

Examples of discovered effective instructions:

  • "Include witty remarks and irony"
  • "Provide structured and systematic answers"
  • "Use specific examples and technical terminology"

These instructions serve to remove the characteristic markers of AI text that detectors relied upon.

Experimental Results

  • Attack Success: 95.72% ASR on gpt-3.5-turbo-0613, with AUROC dropping from 98.23% to 62.49%, effectively neutralizing the detector
  • Supervised Detector Vulnerability: Perplexity-based and non-learning methods like DetectGPT are less vulnerable — learning-based detectors rely more on shortcuts
  • Defense Effectiveness: Augmenting with FAILOpt data improves performance across all datasets and attack types
  • Generalization: Effectiveness confirmed across various generation models, tasks, and attack types

Why It Matters

AI text detection is central to academic integrity and information trustworthiness. This study reveals a fundamental vulnerability of current detectors while also presenting a dual-purpose solution that leverages the same attack technique for defense. It applies the security principle of "know your enemy to defend yourself" to AI detection.
