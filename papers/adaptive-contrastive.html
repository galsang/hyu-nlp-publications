Adaptive Contrastive Decoding in Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Handling Noisy Contexts

EMNLP 2024 Findings
Youna Kim, Hyuhng Joon Kim, Cheonbok Park, Choonghyun Park, Hyunsoo Cho, Junyeob Kim, Kang Min Yoo, Sang-goo Lee, Taeuk Kim

One-Line Summary

A method for adaptively adjusting decoding strategies based on the quality of retrieved documents in RAG

Background & Motivation

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) enhances LLMs by feeding them external documents retrieved at inference time. However, retrieved passages are often noisy, irrelevant, or even contradictory to the correct answer. When a model blindly conditions on such low-quality contexts, generation quality degrades significantly.

Limitation: Standard contrastive decoding applies a fixed penalty to suppress the influence of retrieved context, but a fixed weight is suboptimal — it over-corrects when the context is actually helpful and under-corrects when the context is harmful.

Key Insight: The decoding strategy should adapt dynamically based on how relevant the retrieved context is to the query, applying stronger contrastive correction for noisier contexts and weaker correction for helpful ones.

Method

The paper introduces Adaptive Contrastive Decoding (ACD), which modulates the contrastive weight at each generation step based on context relevance:

1
Context Relevance Estimation
Estimate how relevant and reliable the retrieved context is for the given query, using signals derived from the model's own output distributions.
2
Adaptive Weight Computation
Compute a contrastive weight that varies based on the estimated relevance — a larger weight for noisy contexts (to suppress their influence) and a smaller weight for relevant contexts (to preserve their contribution).
3
Contrastive Decoding
During token generation, contrast the output distribution conditioned on retrieved context against the distribution without context, scaled by the adaptive weight, to produce the final token probabilities.

Results

  • Noise Robustness: ACD significantly outperforms fixed contrastive decoding and standard RAG baselines when retrieved contexts contain irrelevant or misleading information
  • Preserved Gains from Good Retrieval: Unlike fixed contrastive methods that can hurt performance when contexts are helpful, ACD retains the benefits of high-quality retrieved passages
  • Consistent Improvements: Gains are observed across multiple open-domain QA benchmarks, demonstrating the generality of the adaptive approach
  • No Additional Training Required: ACD operates purely at decoding time, making it a plug-and-play enhancement for any existing RAG pipeline

Why It Matters

RAG systems are increasingly deployed in production, but retrieval quality is inherently unpredictable. A system that works well with perfect retrieval can fail catastrophically with noisy results. ACD addresses this fundamental brittleness by making the generation process context-aware at the decoding level, providing a training-free mechanism that makes RAG systems more reliable in real-world conditions where retrieval quality varies widely.
