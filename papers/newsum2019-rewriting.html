Summary Level Training of Sentence Rewriting for Abstractive Summarization

The Second Workshop on New Frontiers in Summarization (NewSum 2019) at EMNLP-IJCNLP 2019
Sanghwan Bae, Taeuk Kim, Jihoon Kim, Sang-goo Lee

One-Line Summary

An improved Sentence Rewriting framework for abstractive summarization that introduces summary-level ROUGE optimization through reinforcement learning and a BERT-based extractor, achieving state-of-the-art results on CNN/Daily Mail and New York Times datasets.

Background & Motivation

Sentence Rewriting models combine extractive and abstractive summarization: an extractor selects salient sentences, then an abstractor paraphrases them into a summary. However, existing models suffer from a critical mismatch:

Training-Evaluation Mismatch: Previous work trains the extractor with sentence-level ROUGE rewards, but the final model is evaluated with summary-level ROUGE scores. Sentences with the highest individual ROUGE scores do not necessarily lead to an optimal summary -- they may overlap or be redundant.

Proposed Method

The model consists of two neural network modules -- an extractor and an abstractor -- with two key improvements:

1
BERT-based Extractor
BERT is adapted as the encoder, with [CLS] tokens inserted before each sentence and interval segment embeddings to handle multi-sentence documents. An LSTM pointer network serves as the decoder for sequential sentence selection.
2
Summary-Level RL Training
Using the Advantage Actor-Critic method, the extractor is trained to directly optimize summary-level ROUGE-L scores. Reward shaping provides intermediate signals at each extraction step to address training signal sparsity.
3
Redundancy Control
Trigram blocking for the extractor and reranking for the abstractor further reduce redundancy in generated summaries.

Experimental Results

Evaluated on CNN/Daily Mail, New York Times (NYT50), and DUC-2002 datasets.

  • New SOTA for Abstractive: BERT-ext + abs + RL + rerank achieves the best abstractive summarization results on CNN/Daily Mail with R-AVG 33.54
  • Strong Extractor: The BERT-based extractor alone (BERT-ext) outperforms all previous extractors in the Sentence Rewriting framework (R-1: 42.29, R-2: 19.38, R-L: 38.63)
  • NYT50: The model achieves R-1: 46.63, R-2: 26.76, R-L: 43.38 on the New York Times dataset
  • DUC-2002 Generalization: Outperforms baselines with large margins (R-1: 43.39, R-2: 19.38, R-L: 40.14), demonstrating strong out-of-domain generalization
  • Human Evaluation: Achieves the highest total score (127) in relevance (66) and readability (61) compared to baselines

Why It Matters

This work addresses a fundamental problem in extractive-abstractive summarization: the gap between sentence-level training and summary-level evaluation. By combining BERT's representation power with summary-level reinforcement learning, the approach achieves state-of-the-art results and demonstrates that globally optimizing the entire summary quality is more effective than locally optimizing individual sentences.
