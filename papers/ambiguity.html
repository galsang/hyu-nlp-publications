Aligning Language Models to Explicitly Handle Ambiguity

EMNLP 2024
Hyuhng Joon Kim, Youna Kim, Cheonbok Park, Junyeob Kim, Choonghyun Park, Kang Min Yoo, Sang-goo Lee, Taeuk Kim

One-Line Summary

A method for aligning language models to explicitly handle ambiguity in questions

Background & Motivation

When users ask questions like "Who won the national championship?", the query can refer to many different championships across different sports and years. Large language models (LLMs) typically pick a single interpretation and answer confidently, ignoring the inherent ambiguity. This leads to responses that may be technically correct for one reading but misleading overall.

Limitation: Existing LLMs tend to produce a single answer even when a question is genuinely ambiguous, failing to surface alternative valid interpretations that the user may have intended.

Key Insight: The degree of perceived ambiguity is model-dependent — a model with broader knowledge recognizes more possible interpretations than one with limited knowledge. Alignment should therefore be tailored to each model's own knowledge boundary.

Method

The paper proposes a four-stage alignment pipeline that teaches an LLM to detect ambiguity and respond with disambiguating clarifications:

1
Ambiguity Filtering
Identify samples that the model cannot already explicitly handle — questions where the model gives a single answer despite genuine ambiguity.
2
Self-Disambiguation & Information Gain
The model self-disambiguates each question, and the information gain from disambiguation is measured to rank which samples are most valuable for training.
3
Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT)
High information-gain samples are used to fine-tune the model, teaching it to produce responses that enumerate possible interpretations.
4
Preference Optimization
Further alignment through preference-based training to reinforce explicit ambiguity handling over single-answer responses.

Results

  • Ambiguity Detection: Aligned models significantly improve their ability to recognize when a question is ambiguous rather than blindly selecting one interpretation
  • Answer Coverage: The aligned models produce responses that cover a broader set of valid answers, addressing multiple plausible interpretations of ambiguous queries
  • Maintained Clarity: On unambiguous questions, the aligned models continue to provide direct, single answers without unnecessary hedging
  • Information-Gain Selection: Using information gain to select training samples proves more effective than random selection, confirming the value of the model-aware data curation strategy

Why It Matters

Real-world user queries are often underspecified or ambiguous, yet most LLMs are trained to give a single decisive answer. This work provides a principled framework for teaching models when to ask for clarification and how to present multiple valid interpretations. By grounding the alignment process in each model's own knowledge, it avoids both over-hedging (flagging clear questions as ambiguous) and under-hedging (ignoring genuine ambiguity), making LLM interactions more trustworthy and informative.
