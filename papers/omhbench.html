OMHBench: Benchmarking Balanced and Grounded Omni-Modal Multi-Hop Reasoning

ACL 2026 Findings
Seunghee Kim, Ingyu Bang, Seokgyu Jang, Changhyeon Kim, Sanghwan Bae, Jihun Choi, Richeng Xuan, Taeuk Kim

One-Line Summary

A benchmark that systematically and in a balanced manner evaluates multi-hop reasoning across three modalities — text, image, and audio — using 6,144 questions.

Background & Motivation

While state-of-the-art AI models claim to handle text, images, and audio simultaneously, their ability to reason across these modalities has not been properly validated. Analyzing existing benchmarks revealed two critical problems:

Problem 1 (OMU benchmarks): "Modality shortcuts" exist that allow 70-80% of questions to be solved without visual or audio inputs

Problem 2 (CMR benchmarks): Balancing reasoning paths causes performance to drop by up to 18%, suggesting that existing results were overestimated

Benchmark Construction Pipeline

1
Table Construction
Compose 3 tables of 10 entities x 3 attributes with shared entities
2
Multi-Hop QA Generation
Generate 3-hop questions by combining 8 operation types including Lookup, Ranking, and Comparison
3
Omni-Modal Conversion
Text (LLM), images (matplotlib/seaborn), audio (Kokoro TTS)
4
Reasoning Path Diversification
Evenly distribute across 6 modality orderings (T-V-S, V-S-T, etc.)

Quality assurance: Entity anonymization, QA-based consistency verification, question rephrasing with 3 LLMs, TTS verification (WER: 0.03, STOI: 99.2)

  • Audio Difficulty: Performance drops significantly as audio appears later in the reasoning path
  • Cross-Domain Gap: Up to 21.8% performance difference between Economics and Nutrition domains
  • Path Balance Score: Most models exhibit low PBS, indicating poor robustness to reasoning path variations

Why It Matters

Building truly "omni-modal" AI requires more than handling each modality independently. OMHBench addresses the modality shortcut and path imbalance issues in existing benchmarks to evaluate genuine cross-modal reasoning ability. By precisely identifying weaknesses in the audio modality, it provides direction for the development of next-generation multimodal AI.
