Self-Guided Contrastive Learning for BERT Sentence Representations

ACL-IJCNLP 2021
Taeuk Kim, Kang Min Yoo, Sang-goo Lee

One-Line Summary

Improving BERT sentence representations through self-guided contrastive learning

Background & Motivation

Pre-trained language models like BERT produce powerful contextual word representations, but deriving high-quality sentence-level representations from them remains challenging. Naive approaches such as averaging token embeddings or using the [CLS] token often yield sentence embeddings that underperform even non-contextual baselines like GloVe on semantic similarity tasks.

Limitation: Existing contrastive learning methods for sentence embeddings rely on external NLI datasets or data augmentation pipelines to construct positive and negative pairs, limiting their applicability when such resources are unavailable.

Innovation: This work proposes using the model's own intermediate-layer representations as a self-supervision signal, eliminating the need for any external data or augmentation to guide contrastive learning.

Method

Self-Guided Contrastive Learning (SG-CL) fine-tunes BERT by contrasting its final-layer sentence representations against its own intermediate-layer representations. The key insight is that different layers of BERT capture different levels of linguistic abstraction, providing a natural source of diverse views for contrastive learning.

  • Self-Guided Positive Pairs: For each sentence, intermediate-layer representations serve as positive targets for the final-layer representation, encouraging consistency across layers
  • In-Batch Negatives: Other sentences within the same mini-batch serve as negatives, pushing apart representations of different sentences
  • No External Data Required: The entire training procedure requires only unlabeled sentences, making it applicable to any domain without NLI or paraphrase data

Results

  • Semantic Textual Similarity: Achieves significant improvements on STS benchmarks over vanilla BERT representations and competitive performance with methods that use external supervision
  • Transfer Tasks: Demonstrates strong performance on downstream transfer tasks including sentiment analysis, question classification, and natural language inference
  • Data Efficiency: Because no labeled data is needed for contrastive fine-tuning, the method is particularly effective in low-resource or domain-specific settings

Why It Matters

This work showed that high-quality sentence representations can be extracted from BERT without relying on curated training pairs or data augmentation. By exploiting the model's own layered structure as a self-supervision signal, SG-CL opened up a practical path toward unsupervised sentence embeddings that later influenced the broader contrastive learning for NLP community, including follow-up works like SimCSE.
