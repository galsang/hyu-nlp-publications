UniKnow: A Unified Framework for Reliable Language Model Behavior across Parametric and External Knowledge

arXiv 2025
Youna Kim, Hyuhng Joon Kim, Minjoon Choi, Sungmin Cho, Hyunsoo Cho, Sang-goo Lee, Taeuk Kim

One-Line Summary

A framework that comprehensively evaluates and addresses 4 scenarios — conflict, distraction, absence, etc. — that arise when LLMs integrate parametric (internal) and external knowledge.

Background & Motivation

LLMs utilize both their internally learned knowledge (parametric knowledge) and externally retrieved knowledge (external knowledge) via RAG and similar methods. However, various situations arise depending on the relationship between these two knowledge sources:

  • Conflict: Internal knowledge and external knowledge contradict each other
  • External-Only: Relevant knowledge exists only in external sources, not in parametric memory
  • Parametric-Only: External knowledge is distracting noise and only internal knowledge is accurate
  • Unknown: No knowledge source has the answer

Core Problem: Existing methods focus on specific scenarios (e.g., only conflicts) and exhibit bias where performance degrades in other scenarios.

Proposed Method

Parametric Knowledge Estimation: For each question, 10 samples are drawn; if 70% or more are correct, relevant PK is deemed present

Context Construction: 4 types — original, conflict-generated, irrelevant random, and inaccurately retrieved contexts enable scenario-specific evaluation

Evaluation Scope: 7 datasets total: NQ, TriviaQA, HotpotQA, SQuAD, BioASQ, TextbookQA, RelationExtraction

Experimental Results

  • LM_UniKnow Achieves Best Performance: Training that covers all scenarios yields the best overall performance — "broader scenario coverage leads to better results"
  • Difficulty of Conflict Resolution: Processing external knowledge that conflicts with existing PK is much harder than integrating new information
  • Answerability vs Reliability Trade-off: Answerability-focused methods excel at P-Only but hallucinate on Unknown; abstention-focused methods show the opposite pattern
  • Scale Effect: As model size increases, abstention ability consistently improves, but improvement on E-Only remains marginal

Why It Matters

In reality, knowledge conflicts, distractions, and absences can occur simultaneously. Rather than handling only one situation well, a comprehensive approach is needed to build AI that is reliable across all knowledge states. UniKnow provides a systematic evaluation framework and training methodology for this purpose.
