FCMR: Robust Evaluation of Financial Cross-Modal Multi-Hop Reasoning

ACL 2025
Seunghee Kim, Changhyeon Kim, Taeuk Kim

One-Line Summary

A benchmark that evaluates multimodal multi-hop reasoning across text reports, tables, and charts in the financial domain at 3 difficulty levels. Even the best-performing model achieves only 30.4% on the Hard level.

Background & Motivation

Real-world financial analysis requires comprehensively reading text reports, verifying numbers in tables, and identifying trends in charts. However, existing benchmarks had two critical problems:

Data Contamination: GPT-4o achieves 43.4% on MMQA without images — suggesting that existing benchmarks may already be included in training data. On FCMR, performance without images drops to 14.71%, significantly reducing contamination risk.

Proposed Method: CMRGen Pipeline

1
Data Construction
SEC EDGAR 10-K reports (text) + WRDS Compustat (tables) + matplotlib/plotly (charts)
2
Question Generation
Trend, Ranking, Conditional, and Arithmetic operation templates
3
Paraphrasing & Filtering
2-stage LLM paraphrasing + bias removal, at $0.004 per question

  • Ceiling of Best Performance: Even Claude 3.5 Sonnet achieves only 30.39% accuracy on Hard
  • Key Bottleneck: The information retrieval stage is the biggest failure factor — finding the right information is the hardest part
  • Error Types: Chart trend interpretation errors (35 cases), ranking judgment mistakes (16 cases), numerical interpretation errors (17 cases)
  • Optimization Potential: Applying modality integration + 4-stage reasoning + Self-Refine improves Hard from 32% to 46%

Why It Matters

Finance is a domain where accuracy is paramount. For AI to assist in financial analysis, it must accurately connect and reason across multiple data formats. FCMR is a benchmark that is robust against data contamination while reflecting real financial settings, with the practical advantage of large-scale generation at only $0.004 per question.
