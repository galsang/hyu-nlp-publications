Think Just Enough: Leveraging Self-Assessed Confidence for Adaptive Reasoning in Language Models

EACL 2026 Findings
Junyeob Kim, Sang-goo Lee, Taeuk Kim

One-Line Summary

A framework that uses Shannon entropy from token-level logprobs as a confidence signal to enable selective early stopping, achieving 25-50% computational savings with zero accuracy degradation.

Background & Motivation

Advanced reasoning models (like o1, QwQ) generate long chains of thought even for simple questions, wasting significant computation. Can we detect when the model is already confident enough and stop early?

Key Insight: Shannon entropy computed from token-level logprobs provides a reliable, model-intrinsic confidence signal that strongly discriminates between questions the model can and cannot answer correctly.

Proposed Method

The framework computes Shannon entropy from the top-20 logprobs after softmax normalization, then applies theoretically grounded thresholds to decide whether to stop reasoning early or continue.

1
Confidence Assessment
Compute Shannon entropy from token logprobs to quantify the model's certainty about its answer
2
Threshold Selection
Apply one of 4 theoretically grounded methods to determine the confidence cutoff
3
Early Stopping
Stop or continue reasoning based on whether confidence exceeds the selected threshold

Four threshold methods:

  • Entropy Mean: Conservative baseline using average entropy as the cutoff
  • Information-Theoretic Optimal: Maximizes information gain for optimal discrimination
  • Bayesian Optimal: Minimizes overall classification error
  • Scale-Invariant Universal: Works across different models without recalibration

The framework requires only 5-10 examples for basic deployment and 25+ examples for full optimization.

Experimental Results

  • 25-50% token reduction across all model-dataset pairs
  • 88-100% accuracy for high-confidence questions
  • Zero accuracy degradation (no loss in overall performance)
  • Cohen's d: 0.66-1.95 (strong effect size for confidence discrimination)
  • Evaluated on GPT OSS 120B/20B, Qwen3-30B across AIME'24, AIME'25, GPQA Diamond

Why It Matters

As reasoning models become mainstream, their computational cost becomes a bottleneck. This work shows we can intelligently allocate compute where it is needed, saving 25-50% of cost with no quality loss. This has direct implications for deploying reasoning models at scale in cost-sensitive production environments.
