OmniACBench: A Benchmark for Evaluating Context-Grounded Acoustic Control in Omni-Modal Models

arXiv 2026
Seunghee Kim, Bumkyu Park, Kyudan Jung, Joosung Lee, Soyoon Kim, Jeonghoon Kim, Taeuk Kim, Hwiyeol Jo

One-Line Summary

A benchmark that evaluates omni-modal models' ability to generate contextually appropriate tone and speech given voice instructions, text scripts, and images, across 6 acoustic dimensions.

Background & Motivation

While state-of-the-art AI models can respond with voice, there is a critical difference between simply reading text aloud and speaking with a tone appropriate to the situation. Just as one's tone should differ when delivering sad news versus offering congratulations, AI must also generate speech that matches the context.

Key Question: Can a model that understands text well and recognizes images also respond appropriately to a request to "read this in the right tone"?

Benchmark Design

6 evaluation dimensions:

  • Speech Rate: Ability to control speaking speed (fast/slow)
  • Phonation: Phonation types such as whispering and shouting
  • Pronunciation: Pronunciation characteristics such as emphasis and intonation
  • Emotion: Emotional expression including joy, sadness, anger, etc.
  • Global Accent: Accents such as British English, American English, etc.
  • Timbre: Voice quality characteristics such as child-like or elderly voices

Benchmark scale: 3,559 instances (97.78% retained from an initial 3,640 after 2-stage quality verification)

3 Failure Patterns:

  • Direct Control Failure: Some properties like timbre cannot be controlled even with explicit instructions
  • Implicit Reasoning Failure: Failure to infer appropriate acoustic properties from context
  • Multimodal Grounding Failure: Success with text alone but failure in multimodal contexts — the core bottleneck

Diagnostic findings: Individually, text generation is perfect (WER 0.09-0.14) and visual cue reasoning achieves 92-97.5% accuracy, but failures occur in the process of integrating these into speech output.

Why It Matters

As voice AI assistants become ubiquitous, not only "what is said" but also "how it is said" becomes important. This benchmark is the first to provide systematic evaluation criteria for generating natural speech that matches the context, and reveals fundamental limitations in current models' "multimodal grounding" capabilities.
