Hyper-CL: Conditioning Sentence Representations with Hypernetworks

ACL 2024
Young Hyun Yoo, Jii Cha, Changhyeon Kim, Taeuk Kim

One-Line Summary

A method for conditionally generating sentence representations via hypernetwork-based contrastive learning

Background

Sentence embeddings are central to many NLP applications, and contrastive learning has driven major advances in their quality. However, standard sentence representations capture a single, fixed view of a sentence's meaning. In practice, similarity between sentences often depends on a specific perspective or condition -- for example, two sentences may be similar in topic but dissimilar in sentiment. Existing architectures face a trade-off:

Bi-encoder: Accurate but requires |S| x |C| encoder forward passes (computationally expensive)

Tri-encoder: Efficient with pre-computed embeddings and caching, but limited interaction between sentence and condition leads to underperformance

Method

Hyper-CL bridges the accuracy-efficiency gap by integrating hypernetworks into a tri-encoder framework, enabling dynamic conditioning without sacrificing caching benefits.

1
Encode Sentence & Condition
Use a shared encoder f to produce embeddings: h_s = f(s) for the sentence and h_c = f(c) for the condition
2
Generate Projection via Hypernetwork
A hypernetwork q transforms the condition embedding into a condition-specific projection matrix: W_c = q(h_c), dynamically creating a condition subspace
3
Condition-Aware Projection
Project sentence embeddings into the condition subspace: h_sc = W_c * h_s, enabling the same sentence to have different representations under different conditions
4
Contrastive Training
Train with task-specific contrastive objectives (MSE + InfoNCE for C-STS; adapted SimKGC for knowledge graph completion)

Efficiency trick: Low-rank decomposition (following LoRA principles) reduces the hypernetwork output from cubic to manageable parameter size, and transformation matrices W_c can be cached since they depend only on conditions.

  • C-STS: Reduces the performance gap between tri-encoder and bi-encoder from 13.3 to 6.05 points
  • Speed: 108x faster than bi-encoder on WN18RR test set (1.93s vs. 209.98s with BERT-large)
  • Low-rank variant: hyper64-cl achieves 38.36 Spearman with only 75M additional parameters, nearly matching the full model
  • Clustering quality: Entropy drops from 0.739 to 0.270 after condition-specific projection, showing effective semantic reorganization

Why It Matters

Hyper-CL demonstrates that sentence embeddings do not need to be static, one-size-fits-all vectors. By introducing hypernetwork-generated projections, the same pre-computed sentence embedding can be dynamically adapted to different perspectives at near-zero additional inference cost. This opens the door to more nuanced NLP applications -- from conditional retrieval to knowledge graph reasoning -- where the notion of "similarity" fundamentally depends on context, all while maintaining the computational efficiency needed for real-world deployment.
