Subgraph-Aware Training of Language Models for Knowledge Graph Completion Using Structure-Aware Contrastive Learning

WWW 2025
Youmin Ko, Hyemin Yang, Taeuk Kim, Hyunjoon Kim

One-Line Summary

A subgraph structure-aware approach for training language models for knowledge graph completion

Background & Motivation

Knowledge Graph Completion (KGC) -- predicting missing links in knowledge graphs -- traditionally relies on structural embedding methods that struggle with sparse entities. Recent work has turned to large language models (LLMs) for their rich semantic understanding, but fine-tuning LLMs is computationally expensive. This paper asks: can we extract useful KGC signals from frozen LLMs by bridging the gap between language representations and graph structure?

Method

  • Subgraph Entity Descriptions: A generator creates textual descriptions of entities based on their local subgraph neighborhoods, bridging the semantic gap between LLM representations and KG structure.
  • Probing Frozen LLMs: Rather than fine-tuning, the method extracts context-aware hidden states from intermediate layers of frozen LLMs using positive and negative triple pairs, preserving computational efficiency.
  • Structure-Aware Contrastive Learning: A data-efficient classifier is trained on the extracted hidden states to perform triple classification, learning to distinguish valid triples from invalid ones while remaining aware of subgraph structure.
  • Sliced Mutual Information: SMI is used to quantify which hidden layers contain the most task-relevant information, enabling targeted feature extraction.

Results

The approach achieves up to 47% improvement over non-fine-tuned LLM baselines while matching the performance of fully fine-tuned models. Notably, it requires 188x less memory and delivers a 26x speedup compared to fine-tuned approaches, making high-quality KGC accessible without expensive GPU resources.

Why It Matters

This work demonstrates that frozen LLMs already encode sufficient knowledge for KGC tasks when properly probed with structure-aware descriptions. It offers a practical, resource-efficient path for applying LLMs to knowledge graph tasks in real-world settings where computational budgets are limited.
