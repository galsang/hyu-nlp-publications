Memorization or Reasoning? Exploring the Idiom Understanding of LLMs

EMNLP 2025
Jisu Kim, Youngwoo Shin, Uiji Hwang, Jihun Choi, Richeng Xuan, Taeuk Kim

One-Line Summary

A systematic study that analyzes whether LLMs simply memorize idioms or genuinely understand them through reasoning, across 64,660 idioms in 6 languages.

Background & Motivation

Idioms such as "It's raining cats and dogs" have meanings that differ from their literal interpretation. When LLMs process such idioms, are they simply memorizing from training data, or are they reasoning from context? To answer this fundamental question, we conduct large-scale experiments across 6 languages.

MIDAS Dataset

  • Scale: 64,660 idioms across 6 languages (English, German, Chinese, Korean, Arabic, Turkish), totaling 70,909 instances
  • Composition: Each idiom includes a figurative meaning and example sentence
  • Evaluation: 5-choice MCQ with similarity-based distractors + continuation task
  • Compositionality Assessment: Compositionality scores on a 1-5 scale assigned via LLM prompting

Key Findings

  • Hybrid Processing: LLMs process idioms through a hybrid approach that combines contextual cues and reasoning, rather than pure memorization or pure reasoning
  • Impact of Memorization: 3.82-15.83% higher accuracy on memorized idioms — with gaps exceeding 10%p for Korean/Turkish
  • Compositionality and Performance: Correctly answered idioms have 14-44% higher compositionality scores, suggesting that component meanings are leveraged for reasoning
  • Superiority of Reasoning Models: The reasoning-specialized model (QwQ) consistently outperforms the base model (Qwen2.5)
  • Double-Edged Nature of Chain-of-Thought: CoT can be detrimental for high-performing languages, suggesting trade-offs in reasoning model training

Why It Matters

Idiom understanding serves as a litmus test for deep language comprehension. The finding that LLMs utilize reasoning rather than mere memorization provides evidence that these models "understand" language meaning to some extent. At the same time, the large performance gaps in low-resource languages point to directions for advancing multilingual AI.
